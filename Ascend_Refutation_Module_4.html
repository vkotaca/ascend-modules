A Bill to Create a Four-Day School Week
SECTION 1.Public schools in the United States shall adopt a four-day school week, reducing the standard school week from five days to four, while maintaining the annual required instructional hours through longer daily sessions.
SECTION 2.A "four-day school week" is defined as a school calendar in which students attend classes four days per week. Daily instructional hours shall be extended to meet total annual hour requirements set by the Department of Education.
SECTION 3.The Department of Education shall oversee implementation, provide guidance to states and districts, and publish annual compliance reports tracking attendance, academic achievement, and teacher retention.
SECTION 4.This legislation will take effect on January 1, 2027.
SECTION 5.All laws in conflict with this legislation are hereby declared null and void.
◆ The Four-Step Refutation Framework
1
Step One
They Say
Briefly and fairly summarize the opponent's argument. Don't strawman — the chamber will notice. One sentence is usually enough.
They Say → "My opponents claim that a four-day school week improves student mental health by reducing burnout."
2
Step Two
We Say
Directly counter the claim. State your counter-argument clearly — this is the heart of your refutation. Be direct and assertive.
We Say → "However, research shows the four-day model actually increases student stress by compressing content into longer, more exhausting daily sessions."
3
Step Three
Because
Support your counter-argument with evidence, reasoning, or logic. This is where you prove your "We Say." A statistic, study, or clear logical chain works best.
Because → "A 2023 RAND Corporation study found that districts using four-day weeks reported a 12% increase in student-reported stress during extended school days, with disproportionate impact on younger learners."
4
Step Four
Therefore
Tell the judge what this means for the round. Connect your refutation back to the voting decision — why does winning this point matter?
Therefore → "Vote against this bill — it doesn't solve the wellbeing problem, it just relocates the harm, making our students more stressed, not less."
